The Unspoken Ache

Six months burned, then faded, leaving ash. My own tears strangers, a confusing crash. They say "just young," but how to make them see The tangled ache, the un-meant agony? Joy turned to stress, a constant, buzzing hum. Reduced to moments, feeling cold and numb. A youthful chaos, trying to pretend, Until the breaking, the abrupt, hard end. Now just the quiet, stark and truly mine. A desperate need for peace, a single sign I'll find my footing, shedding all the pain. Just me, alone, to find myself again.

Heartbreak Healing Solitude

Rain

Rain, do you have feeling? I don't know but you are everything they want to someone else, a quenching of their deepest thirst. Yet, you are a nightmare to the other, an overwhelming flood they'd wish reversed. Rain, I want to be you so bad. You can cry freely, a torrent without shame or fear. You can be mad, storm of wind and cloud, and you can also shout a thunder that all must hear. Rain, you come and go without a word, unbound by expectation's chain. You are a force, both feared and then adored, while I remain constrained by pain.

The Cartography of Us

Love is not lightning, not fire, not fate it is a slow cartographer, drawing shape to our blurred edges where shadows debate, etching soul-maps in the spaces we escape. It is the silence between two breaths held, the hush of knowing without the need to say, a tenderness not shouted, but spelled in the syntax of glances that never stray. It does not promise always, nor ever, nor end it asks instead, “Will you remain while I unravel?” It builds not on vows, but where wounds mend, planting gardens in our rubble, without gravel. Love is both mirror and moth-bitten page, a cathedral of contradiction wrapped in skin. It asks the old questions, regardless of age: Can we be seen? Can we begin again within? It’s not perfection but patient decay how we hold each other through weathered becoming. It’s less about staying, more about the way you listen when my fears start humming. It is art without answer, carved in motion, the sculpture still wet with doubt and clay. It is drowning, softly, in shared emotion, but choosing the tide, choosing to stay. Love is cruel and soft and unspeakably wise a philosopher cloaked in a child's desire. It’s the ache behind truth, the kiss that denies finality, yet sets the soul on fire. Not a destination, but a long, slow look Love is the margin, not the book.

Philosophical Love

A Thousand "Why Did I?"

A quiet "I hate you" forms on my parched tongue, A prayer to the darkness, where my heart has been flung. My friends nod in sorrow, they parrot my plea, "He messed with your head, he stole bits of you, see?" They don't understand that you stripped me so bare, Took every soft memory, hung them in air. And I'll whisper it louder, a choked, broken sound, My hatred for you, where no mercy is found. But the true hate, the burning, corrosive despair, is reserved for the fool who let you walk in there. How can I accuse you, when I'm the one who failed? I opened the gates, every warning curtailed. I welcomed the storm, let it rage through my soul, And now I just shatter, losing all self-control. These self-questions are killing me, tearing me down, "Why did I stay?" "How could I be such a clown?" Each "what if" and "should I" a relentless, sharp sting, A torturous echo, the misery they bring. And the cruelest of ironies, biting and deep, Is the vision of "us" that my bruised heart still keeps. A pathetic, raw longing, for moments long gone, For a lie that I chase, till the first light of dawn. ______________________________________________

Featured Poem

When You Are Near

When you are near, the world turns slow, Like summer rain that gently falls below. Your laugh, a melody I’ve longed to hear, A song that chases every doubt and fear. Your hand in mine, a quiet, steady beat, A rhythm soft, where heart and heartbeat meet. No need for words—your eyes already say What silence whispers every single day. Love is the warmth that fills the coldest night, A fire glowing with steady, gentle light. It’s in the way you smile just for me, A simple glance, a shared eternity. With you, the ordinary turns divine, Every moment sparkles, every breath is fine. No treasure richer, no dream more clear Love is simply this: when you are near.
