How to Read Tarot: A Straightforward Guide

No fluff, no mystical gatekeeping—just a clear, honest, and practical explanation of how to read tarot cards, even if you’ve never touched a deck in your life.

June 2, 2025 6 min read

Introduction

Reading tarot isn’t about predicting the future in some magical way. It’s about learning how to read situations, emotions, and people—starting with yourself.

You don’t need psychic powers or some ancient bloodline. What you need is a tarot deck, a basic understanding of what the cards mean, and the willingness to pay attention.

Understanding the Deck

The tarot has 78 cards, divided into two main parts: the Major Arcana and Minor Arcana.

Major Arcana reflects deep life themes. Cards like The Fool, The Tower, and Death talk about transformation and challenges.

Minor Arcana, split into Cups, Wands, Swords, and Pentacles, talks about everyday matters—emotions, actions, conflicts, and material life. Here’s what the 78 cards mean.

Choosing and Connecting with Your Deck

Pick a deck that feels right. Don't overthink it. It doesn’t need to be famous or traditional—just something that speaks to you.

Spend time with your cards. Shuffle them, stare at them, study the art. Every deck has a voice. You’re learning its language.

Simple Spreads to Get Started

Start with a three-card spread: past, present, future. It’s direct and gives you a clean story arc.

Look at the relationship between the cards. Are they all Cups? Are they clashing? Which card stands out?

Asking Better Questions

The cards don’t respond well to vague or lazy questions. Don’t ask: 'Will I be happy?' Ask: 'What blocks me from joy right now?'

The more honest your question, the more useful the answer. Avoid yes/no questions. Go deeper.

Reading for Yourself vs Others

When reading for yourself, your bias can get in the way. You might twist the meaning to match your hopes.

That’s normal. It takes practice to read clearly. Journaling helps. Write the cards, your interpretation, and return to it later.

Patterns, Intuition, and Growth

Over time, you’ll notice cards repeating. You’ll recognize certain cards show up in moments of crisis, joy, or decision.

That’s tarot talking back. It’s not random. You’re building a visual memory, a symbolic language that grows the more you use it.

Final Thoughts

Tarot is a tool. It won’t fix your life, but it will show you what you’re avoiding.

It’s not about answers—it’s about clarity. Sit with it, be honest, and let the cards reflect who you really are.
